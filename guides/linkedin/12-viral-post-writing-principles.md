# 12 Viral Post Writing Principles to Create Standout LinkedIn Content

Most LinkedIn posts don't flop because the idea is bad or the person writing it doesn't have an interesting story to tell. They flop (200 impressions max) because they're poorly articulated, the messaging is weak and nobody cares.

- The hook has to create tension.
- The story has to build line by line.
- There has to be some emotion at play.
- The post must be accessible to people.
- There must be a high relatability factor.

Without this stuff (+ more), you give people no reason to comment, save or share.

Viral posts follow patterns. There are 12 copywriting techniques to maximise the chances of content going viral (1000+ likes and 25k-100K+ impressions). Every viral post incorporates at least 80% of these principles.

---

## 1. Introduce a Villain

Have I introduced a villain? This is a common enemy between you and your reader (a thing, experience or person) that your reader finds frustrating or stressful. It. Directly call out that pain in the first few lines of the post - position yourself standing alongside your reader. It's best to root your villain in a specific story that brings it to life / shows its impacts, rather than speaking about it in a vacuum. Make it feel real and timely.

---

## 2. Twist the Knife on the Villain

Have I 'twisted the knife' on the villain? What is the impact of this villain on your reader's life? What does it look like? How does it feel? When or where does it appear? What are the consequences of not fixing it i.e. the cost of inaction? The more you vividly describe the villain, the more you bring it to life in a way that your reader can relate to / feel it.

---

## 3. Create Drama Early

Have I created drama in the first few lines of the post? Raise the stakes by teasing some form of conflict, struggle or frustration that people will find entertaining to read. People gravitate and feed off risk, so amplify this early in your post to reel people in. Give just enough so they want to keep reading and find out what happened.

---

## 4. Set Up a Situational Story

Have I set up a situational story? Illustrate your point visually (through words and imagery) to 'show' or set up a scene. Anchor this in time and place using specific details like the location, the date, the setting to make it feel original and unique. Bonus: do this in the hook.

---

## 5. Establish a Strong Character or Protagonist

Have I established a strong character or protagonist in my post? Never speak about your topic in a vacuum. Always build an argument through a specific person (your hero or your villain), so it feels tangible, more immersive and relatable to your reader. The lesson or message you want to share comes towards the end of the post, the point of the first half is to set up a convincing story that is going to articulate your point (& make it more emotive!)

---

## 6. Make the Reader Feel Instantly Seen

Have I made the reader feel instantly seen? Agitating a very close-to-home struggle. Don't talk "about" their problem in isolation. It has to cause a visceral reaction as they read the post. Describe what it feels like to live it and how that could play out in their daily life. Relatability is so important. So poke the things they might've experienced.

---

## 7. Create Disruption

Have I created 'disruption' in my post? Does it include an unexpected shift or sudden, abrupt change of tone to keep the reader engaged? This 'turbulence' can grip readers to the end. Don't state the obvious, use contrarian thinking and challenge authority. Each line needs to 'rehook' people. So if you find yourself losing interest halfway through your post, you need to cut the waffle and add a short, interruptive line or a stark fact or maybe a 'quote' that someone said that suddenly adds tension / gravitas to what you're saying.

---

## 8. Make It Feel Experienced

Have I made my post feel like something I've experienced? It should read like a flow of uninterrupted thought and be written as you speak. Avoid complex words or technical jargon. The post should actually sound like your authentic voice. So use the language you'd use day to day, capitalise certain words for effect and write in your dialect. A story/message rooted in your reality is more powerful than sharing generic advice/claims.

---

## 9. Tease a Dramatic A to B Transformation

Have I teased or demonstrated a dramatic A to B transformation? Point A is a negative place or situation (where you were or your reader currently is) and point B is a positive or aspirational place (where you now are or where you take your reader). Open up that gap. The best stories take you on a journey. So you need high and low moments to express whatever point you're making. Think about the underdog arc! If you're showing a win, highlight where you started - make that part feel extra rubbish / be specific with details - so that when you announce or describe your recent success, it feels even more impressive.

---

## 10. Call Out Ridiculousness

Have I called out 'ridiculousness?' Take a universal frustration/experience and call it out (align yourself on the side of your reader). Then, by the end of the post, ensure you've presented a simple, obvious and better alternative (that nobody could possibly contest). Think about what most people think but are afraid to say. You can be the voice to call out these things, show how silly / frustrating / unacceptable they are and then present a better alternative. You can be the spokesperson on taboo themes.

---

## 11. Use Numeric Manipulation

Have I used numeric manipulation? Reframe numbers to magnify impact and make abstract costs feel immediate and personal. Don't just state a figure - break it down, stretch it over time, or convert it into monthly payments, daily losses, distance, hours, or opportunity cost. Don't say: "I went to a networking event in Dubai." Say: "I flew 5,291km to meet 87 strangers from the internet." Extrapolated, specific numbers increase perceived stakes, create urgency, and make your message more believable.

---

## 12. Make It Accessible to the Masses

Have I made my post accessible to the masses? Choose broad, universal themes when trying to go "viral" e.g. failure, ambition, confidence, careers, leadership, mental health - topics that resonate across ages, industries and seniorities. Write to one person, but pick themes that many people see themselves in. Then differentiate your post from all the rest on this topic by adding a unique entry point/hook: start with a bold opinion, personal story, a specific experience you had, humour, strong POV, or a visual hook. This makes it both relatable by nature but also interesting to read - because it has a specific, never been seen before and original start.
