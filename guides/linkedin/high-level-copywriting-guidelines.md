LinkedIn Copywriting Guidelines
Here’s a step-by-step guide to help copywriters create high-performing LinkedIn content based on the latest algorithm insights:

1. Post Format & Structure
   ✅ Use short sentences (under 12 words per sentence).
   ✅ Break up your text into small paragraphs with white space.
   ✅ No orphan words (don't leave single words hanging on their own line).
   ❌ Avoid dense blocks of text.

2. Hooks (The First 2-3 Sentences)
   The opening two to three sentences are critical to capture attention.
   Make sure the first sentence sparks curiosity, asks a question, or promises value.✨ Tip: Strong hooks can increase reader retention by 30%.

3. Post Length
   Optimal Length: 900 to 1,500 characters.
   Each additional 300 characters may reduce reach by 10%.✨ Tip: Keep it concise while still delivering value.

4. Post Timing
   🗓 Text-only posts: Monday to Friday between 10 am - 12 pm.
   🗓 Text + Image posts: Monday to Friday between 8 am - 10:30 am.
   🗓 Personal story posts: Saturday & Sunday between 8 am - 12 pm.

5. Use Visuals
   ✅ Add relevant images to boost reach by 15-20%.
   📸 Selfies or personal images can boost reach by up to 30%. 📸 Crop images and videos to a 4:5 ratio to maximize screen space.

6. Commenting Strategy
   💬 Reply to all comments within the first hour to increase reach by 40%.
   🕒 Spend at least 30 minutes daily commenting on other posts to boost your profile visibility.
   📌 Pinned Comments: Use pinned comments on your own posts to spark engagement.

7. Carousels (Slideshows)
   📈 Carousels outperform text+image posts by 1.44 times.
   📚 Optimal length: 6-12 slides.

8. Use AI Tools
   ⚙️ Use AI tools like Notion, Canva, or Grammarly to improve formatting, grammar, and design.

9. Avoid These Mistakes
   ❌ Avoid links in your posts.
   ❌ Avoid hashtags.
   ❌ Avoid excessive tagging.
   ❌ Avoid posting more than once per day.
